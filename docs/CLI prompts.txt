Key Decarbonisation Policies (Transport & Freight)
Zero Emission Vehicle (ZEV) Mandate: Crucial for enforcing manufacturer sales targets, requiring 28% of new cars and 16% of vans to be zero-emission in 2025, rising annually.
Phase-out Deadlines: New petrol and diesel cars/vans will be banned by 2030, with all new cars/vans needing to be 100% zero-emission by 2035.
Freight/HGV Decarbonisation: Plans to end the sale of new non-zero emission HGVs: those 3.5–26 tonnes by 2035, and over 26 tonnes by 2040.
Infrastructure Investment: Rapidly expanding public EV charging points, particularly for commercial fleets and HGV charging/refuelling infrastructure.
Renewable Transport Fuel Obligation (RTFO): Encourages renewable fuel use in road and maritime transport.
Rail Electrification & Greening: Commitment to remove all diesel-only trains by 2040, investing in battery and hydrogen technology.
Maritime and Aviation Net Zero: Pushing for net-zero domestic aviation by 2040 and significant emissions reductions in shipping by 2050 through green technology.
Local Authority Support: Implementing last-mile consolidation hubs, promoting, and transitioning local authority fleets to EVs.
